## Why the distinction matters

People often use BMR and RMR interchangeably, and in day-to-day conversation that is common. Still, they are not exactly the same, and the difference matters if you want to understand where calorie estimates come from.

## BMR explained

BMR is the energy your body would use under highly controlled resting conditions. It represents the bare minimum required for basic life functions.

## RMR explained

RMR is similar but measured under less strict real-world conditions. It is often slightly higher than BMR because it is easier to measure outside of laboratory-like settings.

## Which one should you use?

For most readers, the [BMR Calculator](/bmr-calculator) is a practical starting point. Then the [TDEE Calculator](/tdee-calculator) and [Calorie Calculator](/calorie-calculator) turn that baseline into a daily eating target.

In practice, you do not need to get stuck on the terminology before making progress. What matters more is understanding that both numbers are estimates of baseline energy use, and both are only the beginning of a full calorie plan.

## Final takeaway

BMR is measured under stricter conditions, while RMR is measured in a more normal resting state. For most people, the difference is smaller than the difference between a good plan and a bad one, so it makes sense to start with an estimate and adjust from there.
