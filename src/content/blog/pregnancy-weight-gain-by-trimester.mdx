## Why trimester context matters

Weight gain is not usually perfectly even across pregnancy. Looking at it by trimester helps make the pattern less confusing and keeps one unusual week from feeling bigger than it is.

## What influences the range

The recommended range depends heavily on pre-pregnancy BMI and pregnancy context. That is why two people at the same week of pregnancy may be given different guidance and still both be within a healthy range.

## Best use of the calculator

The [Pregnancy Weight Gain Calculator](/pregnancy-weight-gain-calculator) uses pre-pregnancy size and current week to frame the expected range more clearly.

In general, weight gain may be lighter or less predictable early on, then become steadier in the second and third trimesters. That broader pattern is usually more useful than judging one weigh-in by itself.
