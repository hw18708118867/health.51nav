## Why fiber is often overlooked

People track calories and protein far more often than they track fiber, even though fiber has a big effect on digestion, fullness, and overall diet quality. It is one of those parts of the diet that gets noticed most when it is missing.

## Possible signs of low fiber intake

- low fullness after meals
- constipation
- low fruit and vegetable intake
- heavy dependence on refined foods

## How to improve it

Start with the [Fiber Intake Calculator](/fiber-intake-calculator), increase intake slowly, and support the change with the [Water Intake Calculator](/water-intake-calculator).

## Why going too fast can backfire

Increasing fiber too quickly can cause bloating or make the diet feel harder than it needs to. That is why gradual changes tend to work better, especially if fruits, vegetables, legumes, and whole grains have been low for a while.

## Final takeaway

If meals are not very filling, digestion feels off, or plant foods are consistently low, fiber may be part of the reason. Increase it gradually, keep fluids up, and judge the change over a couple of weeks rather than one day.
