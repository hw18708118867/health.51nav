## Why BAC estimates matter

Blood alcohol concentration helps explain why the same number of drinks can affect people very differently. Two people can drink the same amount in one night and still end up with very different levels of impairment and risk.

## Main factors

- body size
- sex
- number of drinks
- drinking duration
- food intake

## What the calculator does

The [BAC Calculator](/bac-calculator) gives a rough estimate, which is useful for education but never for deciding whether driving is safe.

## Why the same drinks do not hit everyone the same way

Alcohol absorption depends on more than the number printed on the bottle. A stronger pour, an empty stomach, a shorter drinking window, or a smaller body size can all push the estimate higher than expected.

That is why people get into trouble when they count drinks but ignore context. The drink count matters, but so do the hours involved and the conditions around the drinking.

## Final takeaway

BAC is shaped by body size, time, food, and drink strength, not just by the number of drinks. Use the [BAC Calculator](/bac-calculator) to understand the pattern, but never use it to justify risky decisions.
