## Why the two numbers confuse people

Blood pressure readings are easy to collect but not always easy to interpret. Many people see two numbers on the screen and know whether they look high or low, but not what each one actually represents.

## What the top number means

Systolic pressure reflects the force in the arteries when the heart beats. It is the top number, and it often gets the most attention because it tends to rise with stress, age, and vascular stiffness.

## What the bottom number means

Diastolic pressure reflects the pressure in the arteries between beats, when the heart is resting and refilling. It gives a different piece of the picture, which is why both numbers matter.
