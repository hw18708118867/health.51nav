## Why sleep duration still matters

Even with good bedtime routines and better sleep habits, total sleep time still matters. It remains one of the strongest levers for recovery, mood, appetite, concentration, and long-term health.

## Typical adult guidance

Most adults do best around seven to nine hours per night, though individual needs can vary. Some people function well near the lower end of that range, while others clearly feel better closer to eight or nine hours.

## Why cycles are not the whole story

Sleep cycles can help with timing, but total duration and sleep consistency usually matter more.

That is why someone can wake at a good point in the cycle and still feel tired if they simply did not get enough sleep. Timing can help, but it cannot fully compensate for chronic short sleep.
