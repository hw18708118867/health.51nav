## Macros make calorie targets easier to use

A calorie target tells you how much to eat. Macro targets help answer the next question, which is what that food should look like across the day. That is why many people find macro planning easier to use than calories alone.

## What the three macros do

- Protein supports muscle repair, recovery, and fullness
- Carbohydrates support training performance and replenish glycogen
- Fat supports hormones, food enjoyment, and nutrient absorption

Every good macro plan still starts with calories, but the macro split changes how that plan feels and performs.

## How macro calculators usually work

A macro calculator typically:

1. Estimates your calorie target
2. Sets protein based on body weight and goal
3. Sets fat at a healthy minimum
4. Assigns the remaining calories to carbohydrates

This is why macro plans often look different for cutting, maintenance, and lean gain phases.

## Why protein usually gets decided first

Protein is the macro most closely tied to muscle retention, recovery, and appetite control. That is why it is often anchored first. After protein is set, carbs and fat can shift more flexibly.
